Description of the Project

Location-Oriented Comprehensive Keychain (L.O.C.K.) was designed and developed as a technical response to these challenges, incorporating advanced technologies such as Global Positioning System (GPS), Geofencing technology, mobile application connectivity, blinkers and buzzer for identification, personal number combination for unlocking via digital keypad and mobile web application, and anti-tampering for preventive measures that provide a sophisticated and strong security framework. This is designed not only for individual convenience and protection of essential belongings but also allows for customizable security settings to ensure a high level of security and prevent unauthorized access. The Incremental Prototyping Model enhances developmental features, emphasizing multiple iterations for early error detection. The study follows a quantitative approach, employing standard methods and statistical procedures to collect and analyze numerical data to develop the L.O.C.K systematically. It utilizes post-evaluation survey questionnaires to gather information based on ISO 25010 to evaluate the device's security, functionality, and portability by three home security expert and 100 travelers to ensure that the L.O.C.K. is a deterrent to secure travelers' belongings.
